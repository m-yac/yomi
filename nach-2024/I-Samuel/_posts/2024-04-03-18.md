- David has gone back to being silent in this chapter – not a single one of his thoughts, feelings, or even private utterances are shared with the reader.
- This is very much in contrast to the people around him: Jonathan falls in love with him at first sight and every paranoid thought of Saul is shared with us as he driven mad with fear of David. Notably we have no idea whether he loves Jonathan back, or whether is intentionally at odds with Saul or is oblivious to it all.
- In contrast to Saul’s periods of opaqueness earlier in this book, David’s does not project weakness and lack of agency. Instead, it heightens our perception of his power, as we are just as much in the dark as Saul as to what his true intentions and plans are.
