- After 2 years, I’m back doing Nach Yomi! I remember exactly where I was when I threw in the towel last time. It was the day after the solar eclipse and I was still fighting the tail end of a nasty virus – I was a bunch of chapters behind, and was sitting sniffling on the couch trying to read them all while my family and friends were packing up to go home (we had all gotten an AirBnB in Vermont). I suddenly lost the motivation to keep going, and then that was it.
- Returning to exactly the spot I left off, I can see why I faltered here. Although I usually find Robert Alter’s translation and commentary thought-provoking and inspiring, here I find it frustrating and lacking.

<!--more-->
- As a queer Jew, I’ve heard countless times about the story of David and Jonathan. Although it was always a given that the text never said it directly, when I heard this story talked about, it always simply *was* a story about two men in love – and a beautiful one at that, as I assumed from the beautiful quotes I heard. I, of course, had never read the story before two years ago (and not again until now), but am having a tough time engaging with it through Alter’s lens.
- I’m finding Alter’s translation cold (I don’t feel myself *believing* the love these characters claim to profess) and with every footnote that goes by I become angrier and angrier that Alter does not seem to even entertain the idea that these men might Love each other with a capital “L.” I wish I could say it was because he thought it was so obvious that it didn’t have to be mentioned, but what I know about him I wouldn’t bet on it.

- Two years ago I remember giving up in anger as I felt like I had to write a post somehow defending why it’s not insane to think that the love these men feel for each other might be the same kind of love as the kind of love everyone would assume a man and a woman are feeling if these exact same words were said about the two of them. Well, after finding myself tempted to walk away from Nach Yomi again for the same reason, I remembered that not only is Mr. Alter not reading this, but I don’t owe an explanation to anyone. Instead, I’ll just let a few of the moments that struck me the most speak for themselves.
