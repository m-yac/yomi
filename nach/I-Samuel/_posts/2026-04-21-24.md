- I’m suddenly realizing that I’m finally learning about some of the events in David’s life mentioned in the Psalms! For some reason it was the mention of the wilderness of {{ 'עין גדי' | he: 'Ein Gedi' }} that put this thought in my head, but as far as I can tell no Psalm explicitly mentions it as its subject.
- I wrote a script to go through all the Psalms, grab their first couple lines, and show me only those that used the word “when” or “while.” Out of those, I checked if any of the events mentioned in them occurred in roughly these chapters – and I found a handful!
<!--more-->
- (As I was writing this page up, I was reminded how challenging the task of translating the Psalms can be! Because they use such archaic and/or poetic language, there are many words and phrases which simply have no known translation – especially the terms used at the beginning of the Psalms which are presumably technical terms for styles of music or types of instruments.)

- There is one psalm that definitely references Chapter 19, specifically {{ 'I Samuel 19:11' | sefaria }}.

{% verses %}
{% vhe לַמְנַצֵּ֣חַ אַל־תַּשְׁחֵת֮ לְדָוִ֢ד מִ֫כְתָּ֥ם בִּשְׁלֹ֥חַ שָׁא֑וּל וַֽיִּשְׁמְר֥וּ אֶת־הַ֝בַּ֗יִת לַהֲמִיתֽוֹ׃ %}
{% vtr For the leader, <i>al tashchet</i>; A <i>michtam</i> of David, when Saul sent [men] and they guarded the house [in order] to kill him. %}
{% endverses Psalm 59:1 %}

- There are two psalms that definitely reference Chapter 21, specifically {{ 'I Samuel 21:11-16' | sefaria }}. This is when David fled to the Philistine city of Gath and had to feign insanity after being taken before its king. Notably, the name of this king is Achish in the text, but Abimelech here – though some argue the latter name can also be used as a generic title for Philistine kings.
{% verses %}
{% vhe לַמְנַצֵּ֤חַ ׀ עַל־י֬וֹנַת אֵ֣לֶם רְ֭חֹקִים לְדָוִ֣ד מִכְתָּ֑ם בֶּאֱחֹ֨ז אוֹת֖וֹ פְלִשְׁתִּ֣ים בְּגַֽת׃ %}
{% vtr For the leader, on <i>yonat eilem r’chokim</i>; A <i>michtam</i> of David, when the Philistines seized him in Gath. %}
{% endverses Psalm 56:1 %}
{% verses %}
{% vhe לְדָוִ֗ד בְּשַׁנּוֹת֣וֹ אֶת־טַ֭עְמוֹ לִפְנֵ֣י אֲבִימֶ֑לֶךְ וַ֝יְגָרְשֵׁ֗הוּ וַיֵּלַֽךְ׃ %}
{% vtr Of David, when he altered his judgment before Abimelech, who drove him out, and [so] he went. %}
{% endverses Psalm 34:1 %}

- There is one psalm that definitely references Chapter 22, specifically {{ 'I Samuel 22:9' | sefaria }}.
{% verses %}
{% vhe לַמְנַצֵּ֗חַ מַשְׂכִּ֥יל לְדָוִֽד׃ בְּב֤וֹא ׀ דּוֹאֵ֣ג הָאֲדֹמִי֮ וַיַּגֵּ֢ד לְשָׁ֫א֥וּל וַיֹּ֥אמֶר ל֑וֹ בָּ֥א דָ֝וִ֗ד אֶל־בֵּ֥ית אֲחִימֶֽלֶךְ׃ %}
{% vtr For the leader; A <i>maskil</i> of David, when Doeg the Edomite came and told Saul, saying: “David has come to the house of Achimelech.” %}
{% endverses Psalm 52:1-2 %}

- There is one Psalm that definitely references Chapter 23, specifically {{ 'I Samuel 23:19' | sefaria }}.
{% verses %}
{% vhe לַמְנַצֵּ֥חַ בִּנְגִינֹ֗ת מַשְׂכִּ֥יל לְדָוִֽד׃ בְּבֹ֣א הַ֭זִּיפִים וַיֹּאמְר֣וּ לְשָׁא֑וּל הֲלֹ֥א דָ֝וִ֗ד מִסְתַּתֵּ֥ר עִמָּֽנוּ׃ %}
{% vtr For the leader, with music; A <i>maskil</i> of David, when the Ziphites came and said to Saul, “Is David not hiding with us?” %}
{% endverses Psalm 54:1-2 %}

- There is one Psalm that references more generally Chapters 23 and 24, see {{ 'I Samuel 23:14-15' | sefaria }} and {{ 'I Samuel 24:2' | sefaria }} for David being referred to as being “in the wilderness” in different places in Judea.
{% verses %}
{% vhe מִזְמ֥וֹר לְדָוִ֑ד בִּ֝הְיוֹת֗וֹ בְּמִדְבַּ֥ר יְהוּדָֽה׃ %}
{% vtr A psalm of David, when he was in the wilderness of Judah. %}
{% endverses Psalm 63:1 %}

- Finally, there are two psalms that either reference Chapter 22 or 24, specifically either {{ 'I Samuel 22:1' | sefaria }} or {{ 'I Samuel 24:4' | sefaria }} - both involve times when David hides in a cave from Saul. The phrase {{ 'אַל־תַּשְׁחֵת' | he: 'al tashchet' }}, which literally means ‘do not destroy,’ is of unknown meaning here and only appears elsewhere in Psalms 58 (which some say is a continuation of this psalm), Psalm 59 (above), and Psalm 75. Interestingly, the Rabbinic sages in {{ 'Midrash Tehillim 58:1' | sefaria }} link this to when David says the same phrase ({{ 'I Samuel 26:9' | sefaria }}) in reference to not killing Saul in a parallel story to the one in Chapter 24.
{% verses %}
{% vhe לַמְנַצֵּ֣חַ אַל־תַּ֭שְׁחֵת לְדָוִ֣ד מִכְתָּ֑ם בְּבׇרְח֥וֹ מִפְּנֵי־שָׁ֝א֗וּל בַּמְּעָרָֽה׃ %}
{% vtr For the leader, <i>al tashchet</i>; A <i>michtam</i> of David, when he fled from before Saul into the cave. %}
{% endverses Psalm 57:1 %}
{% verses %}
{% vhe מַשְׂכִּ֥יל לְדָוִ֑ד בִּֽהְיוֹת֖וֹ בַמְּעָרָ֣ה תְפִלָּֽה׃ %}
{% vtr A <i>maskil</i> of David, when he was in the cave; a prayer. %}
{% endverses Psalm 142:1 %}

- I couldn’t help but notice the run of Psalms 52, 54, 56, 57, and 59 which all specifically reference events of I Samuel. Psalm 51 is not far off, apparently referencing an event in II Samuel 12.
- The only psalm out of these which appears in any major way in regular Jewish ritual is Psalm 34. It is sometimes recited in its entirety in the prelude to the morning service, but one line of it is immediately recognizable as what Jews recite when the Torah is taken from the ark:

{% vhe גַּדְּל֣וּ לַיהֹוָ֣ה אִתִּ֑י %}
{% vtl Gadlu l’Adonai iti, %}
{% vtr Magnify <i>Adonai</i> with me, %}
{% brverses %}
{% vhe וּנְרוֹמְמָ֖ה שְׁמ֣וֹ יַחְדָּֽו׃ %}
{% vtl un’rom’mah sh’mo yachdav. %}
{% vtr and may we elevate His name together. %}
{% endverses Psalm 34:4 %}
